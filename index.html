About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at Washington State University. My general research interests are in the field of the robustness of machine learning systems. My current research focuses on developing efficient learning and optimization algorithms to improve the reliability and safety of deep learning algorithms for diverse settings and data spaces. My current work includes:

  • Robust deep learning for the time-series domain with diverse applications including human activity recognition and mobile health.
  • Trustworthy machine learning for sequential data witha focus on Out-Of-Distribution detection.
  • Uncertainty quantification for reliable machine-learning using conformal prediction.

Projects

Robustness against Adversarial Time-Series

The vulnerability of ML models against adversarial examples is a well-established threat for safety-critical applications. One way to ensure the reliability of deployed ML systems in the real world is to understand these attacks. But aside from Images and Text, do we know how time-series data is affected by adversarial examples?

*Depiction of real-world data affected by noise.

If the sensor used to collect data is affected by noise, is the commonly used Minkowski metric appropriate to compare the similaity of the signals? How does time-series natural perturbation such as time-shifts and frequency-distortion affect the ability of the ML model to classify correctly the input. Check-out our recent AAAI articles to find out more about this problem. To dive into more detail of this problem, check ...

*Depiction of real-world data affected by sensor rotation.

In real-world, the sensors is not only affected by additive noise. Its relative position (such as its angular position on the wrist) can also affect the acquired data. Such position distortion yields signals that are not similar according to Minkowski metric. Therefore, how can we enhance the robustness of deep models to recognize correctly the distorted inputs. Check-out our recent JAIR and ICCAD articles to find out more about this problem.

Out-Of-Distribution (OOD) detection for Safety-critical application

While deep models are successful in reaching high accuracy for many challenging tasks, they remain unpredictable on unseen examples. In many cases where the input originates from unusual events or tail risks, they can make highly-confident predictions that are completely incorrect.

Uncertainty quantification for reliable machine-learning

A reliable ML system is required to not only make the correct predictions but also quantify the uncertainty in its predictions.
