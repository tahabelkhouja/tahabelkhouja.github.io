About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at Washington State University. My general research interests are in the field of the robustness of machine learning systems. My current research focuses on developing efficient learning and optimization algorithms to improve the reliability and safety of deep learning algorithms for diverse settings and data spaces. My current work includes:

  • Robust deep learning for the time-series domain with diverse applications including human activity recognition and mobile health.
  • Trustworthy machine learning for sequential data with a focus on Out-Of-Distribution detection.
  • Uncertainty quantification for reliable machine-learning using conformal prediction.

Research Projects

Adversarial Robustness against Time-Series Perturbations

The vulnerability of ML models against adversarial perturbations is a well-established threat to safety-critical applications. One way to ensure the reliability of deployed ML systems in the real world is to understand these perturbations. But aside from Images and Text, do we know how time-series data (e.g., sensor acquisition, weather data) is affected by adversarial perturbations?

*Depiction of real-world data affected by noise.

When the data collected is perturbed, is the commonly used Minkowski distance appropriate to compare the similarity of the signals? Time-series natural perturbations such as time-shifts and frequency-distortion affect significantly the ability of the ML model to classify correctly the input. How can we enhance the robustness of these classifiers using adversarial perturbations?
Related papers: RO-TS (AAAI'22) & DTW-AR

*Depiction of real-world data affected by sensor rotation.


Time-series data acquisition is prone to natural perturbations (such as hardware orientation or amplified noise) that affect significantly the data. Such distortion yields signals that are dissimilar according to Minkowski or elastic distances. Therefore, how can we enhance the robustness of deep models to recognize correctly the distorted inputs?
Related papers: TSA-STAT (JAIR'22) & StatOpt (ICCAD'22)

Out-Of-Distribution (OOD) Detection for Safety-Critical Application

While deep-learning models are successful in reaching high accuracy for many challenging tasks, they remain unpredictable on unseen examples. In many cases where the input originates from unusual events or tail risks, they can make highly-confident predictions that are completely incorrect. OOD detection improves the predictive uncertainty of deep-learning models that is essential for real-world contexts with low tolerance for error.
Related papers: SR Score

Uncertainty Quantification for Reliable Machine-Learning


Safe deployment of deep neural networks in high-stake real-world applications require theoretically-sound uncertainty quantification. We study Conformal Prediction for uncertainty quantification of deep models to obtain prediction sets for classification/regression where the correct prediction is contained with high probability.
